01 · Abstract

Joint Speech & Motion from Live Audio

Human communication seamlessly integrates speech with non-verbal cues such as gestures and body posture to convey meaning and intent. Existing voice-motion systems rely on fragmented pipelines that introduce cascaded errors and sentence-level latency. We present TTM, the first end-to-end multimodal language model for joint speech and full-body motion generation from live audio with sub-second latency. Its Thinker-Talker-Mover architecture simultaneously drives speech synthesis and motion generation from a shared audio encoding, eliminating cross-stage errors.


We propose a plug-and-play token-length-aware bucketing strategy for accelerating a Mixture-of-Experts module, named BucketMoE, which achieves an ~8× speedup in both training and inference. We further introduce a VQ-VAE codebook-based motion augmentation method named TopK, which enhances both audio-motion alignment and the robustness of the autoregressive generation process, leading to ~7% improvement in overall performance. Experiments demonstrate state-of-the-art co-speech motion quality with real-time responsiveness.

⛓️

Problem I: No Unified Model

Existing systems chain speech recognition → LLM → TTS → motion generation independently. Errors cascade silently with no joint optimization.

⏱️

Problem II: Sentence-Level Latency

Current systems buffer entire utterances before producing output, incurring 1.5–3s latency. Natural turn-taking demands sub-second response times.

📊

Problem III: Limited Data

BEAT (~60h) and HumanML3D (~28h) are small by modern standards. Random temporal cropping produces semantically incomplete fragments.

03 · Method

Thinker · Talker · Mover

TTM extends Qwen3-Omni with a three-component framework that unifies perception, language reasoning, speech synthesis, and motion generation within a single computation graph.

🧠

Thinker

Encodes live audio into a hierarchy of hidden representations at multiple layer depths. Its final hidden state conditions the Talker; an intermediate state at layer 24 routes to the Mover — enabling motion gradients to back-propagate into audio encoding.

🗣️

Talker

A frozen speech synthesis module driven by the Thinker's final-layer representation. Outputs are encoded via RVQ with 8 codebooks and decoded by Code2Wav. Its intermediate representations actively condition the Mover via cross-modal distillation.

🏃

Mover

Generates full-body motion tokens autoregressively by conditioning on both Thinker and Talker hidden states via a trainable Mover Connector. Motion is represented in HumanML3D format with corrected SMPL rotation parameters for direct robot retargeting.

Key Innovations
01

Cross-Modal Intermediate Coupling

Rather than conditioning downstream modules only on the Thinker's final output, TTM routes an intermediate-layer representation simultaneously to both the Talker and Mover. During training, motion gradients back-propagate through this representation into the Thinker — a feedback loop structurally impossible in any cascaded pipeline.

02

BucketMoE: ~8× MoE Acceleration

After standard expert dispatch, tokens are grouped into K length-aware buckets and processed as a single batched matrix operation. Reduces sequential iterations from N=128 to K=8 (training) or K=1 (decode). Achieves ~8× wall-clock speedup with no architectural changes, no extra memory, and no code modifications — a pure runtime patch.

03

TopK Motion Augmentation

Rather than committing to the single nearest VQ-VAE codebook entry, TTM samples uniformly from the k=5 nearest entries. These motion synonyms expose the Mover to a richer training distribution while preserving audio-motion temporal alignment by construction, yielding an average ~7% performance gain.
